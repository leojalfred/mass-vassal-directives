[h1]Leo VI's Mass Vassal Directives[/h1]

Stop clicking through every vassal one by one. Write the rules once, and let the mod hand out the directives.

[img]https://images.steamusercontent.com/ugc/11939547504358300124/D669A9083C62B3259B9B48620881481791A86FEB/?imw=5000&imh=5000&ima=fit&impolicy=Letterbox&imcolor=%23000000&letterbox=false[/img]

[h2]How it works[/h2]

You give the mod a [b]running order[/b]. Each vassal gets the first directive in it that suits them. A vassal no rule claims is simply left alone.

A priority is one of three things:
[list]
[*][b]Assign a Directive[/b]: give it to every vassal who reaches this far.
[*][b]Check a Condition[/b]: ask something about the vassal, and send each answer its own way. Either branch can ask again, so one priority can weigh two questions.
[*][b]Continue to Next Priority[/b]: do nothing here, and let the next one decide.
[/list]

The trick to reading a rule set: a vassal who [i]cannot[/i] be given a directive falls through to the next priority. That is the game's own doing (it never offers a directive it would not allow), so a rule can say "improve development" without you worrying about the vassals who cannot.

[h2]Presets[/h2]

[img]https://images.steamusercontent.com/ugc/12016510304585716709/B19D8CC9EC226F54B6F65BF4E5A46269F7C28F72/?imw=5000&imh=5000&ima=fit&impolicy=Letterbox&imcolor=%23000000&letterbox=false[/img]

Pick one and its plan appears in the panel, so you can read what it actually does rather than take the name on trust.

[list]
[*][b]None[/b]: nothing is assigned. The default: the mod does nothing until you choose.
[*][b]Convert and Develop[/b]: faith, then culture, then growth. The strong develop their land, the weak build up their military.
[*][b]Unify the Realm[/b]: converts harder, at the cost of growth.
[*][b]Prepare for War[/b]: arms the realm, each vassal to their strength.
[*][b]Grow the Economy[/b]: wealth first. The undeveloped develop, the established build.
[*][b]Custom[/b]: your own rules.
[/list]

[b]A preset is a starting point, not a take-it-or-leave-it.[/b] Change any of its rules and the whole thing becomes yours, exactly as it was, plus your change. Your own rules are kept aside while a preset is loaded, so trying one never costs you your work.

[h2]Custom rules[/h2]

[img]https://images.steamusercontent.com/ugc/12790412026001156687/1AF46F57AB0B989D3DB190D86152F0CFD2E66801/?imw=5000&imh=5000&ima=fit&impolicy=Letterbox&imcolor=%23000000&letterbox=false[/img]

Up to six priorities, plus three for nomads, judged on any of fourteen conditions:

Faith is Yours · Culture is Yours · Holds Counties of Another Faith · Holds Counties of Another Culture · Same Dynasty as You · Administrative Government · Is a Powerful Vassal · Is on Your Council · Military Strength · Title Tier · Capital Development · Opinion of You · Counties Held · Cultural Acceptance with You

Nomads keep a running order of their own: they have four directives no one else can be given, and can be given none of the other nine.

[h2]Using it[/h2]

[olist]
[*]Open the [b]Realm[/b] window, go to [b]Subjects[/b], and click the directives button in the header, next to [i]Toggle Compact List[/i]. The panel appears alongside. Drag it wherever you like.
[*]Choose a preset. [b]Nothing is assigned until you do.[/b]
[*]Tick [b]Automatically Reassign Vassal Directives[/b] to run the rules every month and again whenever you change anything. Or leave it off and use [b]Apply Now[/b] when you feel like it.
[/olist]

[img]https://images.steamusercontent.com/ugc/14607102700321109230/685714B54F475E5CEA3584436BE612E1F16878D7/?imw=5000&imh=5000&ima=fit&impolicy=Letterbox&imcolor=%23000000&letterbox=false[/img]

[b]Want to handle someone yourself?[/b] Right-click their portrait and choose [b]Exempt from Directive Automation[/b]. They are passed over entirely, and their directive icon shows dimmed so you can see at a glance who you are managing by hand. Or just re-assign a vassal's directive by hand after the mod has set one, and it exempts them for you and steps back.

[b]Changed your mind about all of it?[/b] [b]Remove All[/b] clears every directive the mod assigned and turns it off. Anything you assigned by hand is left alone. The mod only ever clears a directive it recognizes as its own.

[h2]The fine print[/h2]

[list]
[*][b]Nothing about eligibility changes.[/b] The mod calls the game's own rules and mirrors the Give Vassal Directive interaction exactly. It only ever does what you could do by hand.
[*][b]No DLC required, every expansion supported.[/b] The administrative directives come from Roads to Power and nomad vassals from Khans of the Steppe; the mod folds in whatever you own and quietly hides the rest, so the panel and presets only ever show what your game can do.
[*][b]Achievements still work.[/b] Since 1.9, mods do not disable them.
[*][b]Safe to add to an existing save[/b], and safe to remove from one.
[*][b]Multiplayer[/b]: every player gets their own rules, on their own character.
[*][b]The AI is untouched.[/b] Your rules are yours; AI rulers manage their own vassals as always.
[*][b]Compatibility[/b]: the panel is a standalone window and overrides no vanilla interface file. The mod replaces no vanilla file at all; it only redefines two named text functions so an exempt vassal's icon can be dimmed, and conflicts only with other mods that redefine those same two.
[*][b]A Game of Thrones[/b]: a [url=https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3767330440]separate build of this mod[/url] is made for the A Game of Thrones total conversion, with its Settle Wilderness directive, Westeros conditions, and thresholds tuned to its larger armies. Play [url=https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3767330440]that one[/url] alongside AGOT instead of this one.
[*][b]Herders[/b] get no directives in the base game, so they get none here either.
[*][b]Every official language[/b] is supported. The English is written by hand; the rest are machine-translated, so please forgive any stiffness, and corrections are welcome.
[/list]

[b]Warning:[/b] while automation is on, the rules can overwrite a directive you set by hand on a vassal the mod is not already managing. But change a directive the automation itself gave a vassal, and it exempts them for you, so your choice stands. To protect a vassal from the start, exempt them yourself.

[h2]Bugs and contributions[/h2]

I am just one person and this mod is a hobby project, so please be patient if a patch breaks something. Bug reports are welcome. An [i]error.log[/i] excerpt and what you expected goes a long way. Contributions to the mod through its [url=https://github.com/leojalfred/mass-vassal-directives]public GitHub repository[/url] are very welcome too, including fixes to the machine-made translations.

[b]Requires:[/b] Crusader Kings III 1.19.x. No DLC is needed, and every expansion is supported.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Stop clicking through every vassal one by one. Write the rules once, and let the mod hand out the directives.


HOW IT WORKS

You give the mod a running order. Each vassal gets the first directive in it that suits them. A vassal no rule claims is simply left alone.

A priority is one of three things:

• Assign a Directive: give it to every vassal who reaches this far.
• Check a Condition: ask something about the vassal, and send each answer its own way. Either branch can ask again, so one priority can weigh two questions.
• Continue to Next Priority: do nothing here, and let the next one decide.

The trick to reading a rule set: a vassal who cannot be given a directive falls through to the next priority. That is the game's own doing (it never offers a directive it would not allow), so a rule can say "improve development" without you worrying about the vassals who cannot.


PRESETS

Pick one and its plan appears in the panel, so you can read what it actually does rather than take the name on trust.

• None: nothing is assigned. The default: the mod does nothing until you choose.
• Convert and Develop: faith, then culture, then growth. The strong develop their land, the weak build up their military.
• Unify the Realm: converts harder, at the cost of growth.
• Prepare for War: arms the realm, each vassal to their strength.
• Grow the Economy: wealth first. The undeveloped develop, the established build.
• Custom: your own rules.

A preset is a starting point, not a take-it-or-leave-it. Change any of its rules and the whole thing becomes yours, exactly as it was, plus your change. Your own rules are kept aside while a preset is loaded, so trying one never costs you your work.


CUSTOM RULES

Up to six priorities, plus three for nomads, judged on any of fourteen conditions:

Faith is Yours · Culture is Yours · Holds Counties of Another Faith · Holds Counties of Another Culture · Same Dynasty as You · Administrative Government · Is a Powerful Vassal · Is on Your Council · Military Strength · Title Tier · Capital Development · Opinion of You · Counties Held · Cultural Acceptance with You

Nomads keep a running order of their own: they have four directives no one else can be given, and can be given none of the other nine.


USING IT

1. Open the Realm window, go to Subjects, and click the directives button in the header, next to Toggle Compact List. The panel appears alongside. Drag it wherever you like.
2. Choose a preset. Nothing is assigned until you do.
3. Tick Automatically Reassign Vassal Directives to run the rules every month and again whenever you change anything. Or leave it off and use Apply Now when you feel like it.

Want to handle someone yourself? Right-click their portrait and choose Exempt from Directive Automation. They are passed over entirely, and their directive icon shows dimmed so you can see at a glance who you are managing by hand. Or just re-assign a vassal's directive by hand after the mod has set one, and it exempts them for you and steps back.

Changed your mind about all of it? Remove All clears every directive the mod assigned and turns it off. Anything you assigned by hand is left alone. The mod only ever clears a directive it recognizes as its own.


THE FINE PRINT

• Nothing about eligibility changes. The mod calls the game's own rules and mirrors the Give Vassal Directive interaction exactly. It only ever does what you could do by hand.
• No DLC required, every expansion supported. The administrative directives come from Roads to Power and nomad vassals from Khans of the Steppe; the mod folds in whatever you own and quietly hides the rest, so the panel and presets only ever show what your game can do.
• Achievements still work. Since 1.9, mods do not disable them.
• Safe to add to an existing save, and safe to remove from one.
• Multiplayer: every player gets their own rules, on their own character.
• The AI is untouched. Your rules are yours; AI rulers manage their own vassals as always.
• Compatibility: the panel is a standalone window and overrides no vanilla interface file. The mod replaces no vanilla file at all; it only redefines two named text functions so an exempt vassal's icon can be dimmed, and conflicts only with other mods that redefine those same two.
• A Game of Thrones: a separate build of this mod is made for the A Game of Thrones total conversion, with its Settle Wilderness directive, Westeros conditions, and thresholds tuned to its larger armies. Play that one alongside AGOT instead of this one: https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=3767330440
• Herders get no directives in the base game, so they get none here either.
• Every official language is supported. The English is written by hand; the rest are machine-translated, so please forgive any stiffness, and corrections are welcome.

Warning: while automation is on, the rules can overwrite a directive you set by hand on a vassal the mod is not already managing. But change a directive the automation itself gave a vassal, and it exempts them for you, so your choice stands. To protect a vassal from the start, exempt them yourself.


BUGS AND CONTRIBUTIONS

I am just one person and this mod is a hobby project, so please be patient if a patch breaks something. Bug reports are welcome. An error.log excerpt and what you expected goes a long way. Contributions to the mod through its public GitHub repository are very welcome too, including fixes to the machine-made translations: https://github.com/leojalfred/mass-vassal-directives

Requires: Crusader Kings III 1.19.x. No DLC is needed, and every expansion is supported.

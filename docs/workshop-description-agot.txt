[h1]Leo VI's Mass Vassal Directives (A Game of Thrones)[/h1]

Stop clicking through every vassal one by one. Write the rules once, and let the mod hand out the directives. This is the [b]A Game of Thrones[/b] build: same automation, adapted for Westeros.

[b]This is the version to use with A Game of Thrones.[/b] For plain Crusader Kings III, use the base-game version instead. Load this mod after A Game of Thrones in your playset (see the fine print on why).

[img]SCREENSHOT_1_URL[/img]

[h2]How it works[/h2]

You give the mod a [b]running order[/b]. Each vassal gets the first directive in it that suits them. A vassal no rule claims is simply left alone.

A priority is one of three things:
[list]
[*][b]Assign a Directive[/b]: give it to every vassal who reaches this far.
[*][b]Check a Condition[/b]: ask something about the vassal, and send each answer its own way. Either branch can ask again, so one priority can weigh two questions.
[*][b]Continue to Next Priority[/b]: do nothing here, and let the next one decide.
[/list]

The trick to reading a rule set: a vassal who [i]cannot[/i] be given a directive falls through to the next priority. That is the game's own doing (it never offers a directive it would not allow). It is why a rule can tell the whole realm to improve development without you worrying about the feudal lords who cannot, for instance: they simply fall through.

[h2]Made for Westeros[/h2]

[list]
[*][b]Settle the Wilderness[/b]: AGOT's colonization directive is a first-class option, for pushing your frontier lords to settle the empty land they hold.
[*][b]Westeros conditions[/b]: sort your vassals by whether they are Ironborn, or follow the Faith of the Seven, the Old Gods, or R'hllor.
[*][b]The Free Cities[/b] keep their administrative directives (Improve Development, Train Commanders, Build Men-at-Arms) and the Free City condition, so a Braavosi or Pentoshi ruler has real use for them.
[*][b]Tuned for AGOT's armies[/b]: the Military Strength thresholds are set for A Game of Thrones' larger levies, so "a strong vassal" still means one.
[/list]

[h2]Presets[/h2]

[img]SCREENSHOT_2_URL[/img]

Pick one and its plan appears in the panel, so you can read what it actually does rather than take the name on trust.

[list]
[*][b]None[/b]: nothing is assigned. The default: the mod does nothing until you choose.
[*][b]Convert and Develop[/b]: faith, then culture, then growth.
[*][b]Unify the Realm[/b]: converts harder, at the cost of growth.
[*][b]Prepare for War[/b]: arms the realm, each vassal to their strength.
[*][b]Grow the Economy[/b]: wealth first. The undeveloped develop, the established build.
[*][b]Master of the Seven Kingdoms[/b]: a mixed realm, each to their place. Your faith converts, the Ironborn arm, and the rest build by their strength: the strong grow their economy, the weak fortify.
[*][b]Settle the Frontier[/b]: grow the realm and push its borders outward. Your faith converts, the Ironborn arm, and everyone else settles the wilderness they hold.
[*][b]Custom[/b]: your own rules.
[/list]

[b]A preset is a starting point, not a take-it-or-leave-it.[/b] Change any of its rules and the whole thing becomes yours, exactly as it was, plus your change. Your own rules are kept aside while a preset is loaded, so trying one never costs you your work.

[h2]Custom rules[/h2]

[img]SCREENSHOT_3_URL[/img]

Up to six priorities, judged on any of these conditions:

Faith is Yours · Culture is Yours · Holds Counties of Another Faith · Holds Counties of Another Culture · Same Dynasty as You · Free City · Is a Powerful Vassal · Is on Your Council · Military Strength · Title Tier · Capital Development · Opinion of You · Counties Held · Cultural Acceptance with You · Is Ironborn · Follows the Faith of the Seven · Follows the Old Gods · Follows R'hllor

[h2]Using it[/h2]

[olist]
[*]Open the [b]Realm[/b] window, go to [b]Subjects[/b], and click the directives button in the header, next to [i]Toggle Compact List[/i]. The panel appears alongside. Drag it wherever you like.
[*]Choose a preset. [b]Nothing is assigned until you do.[/b]
[*]Tick [b]Automatically Reassign Vassal Directives[/b] to run the rules every month and again whenever you change anything. Or leave it off and use [b]Apply Now[/b] when you feel like it.
[/olist]

[img]SCREENSHOT_4_URL[/img]

[b]Want to handle someone yourself?[/b] Right-click their portrait and choose [b]Exempt from Directive Automation[/b]. They are passed over entirely, and their directive icon shows dimmed so you can see at a glance who you are managing by hand. Or just re-assign a vassal's directive by hand after the mod has set one, and it exempts them for you and steps back.

[b]Changed your mind about all of it?[/b] [b]Remove All[/b] clears every directive the mod assigned and turns it off. Anything you assigned by hand is left alone. The mod only ever clears a directive it recognizes as its own.

[h2]The fine print[/h2]

[list]
[*][b]Nothing about eligibility changes.[/b] The mod calls the game's own rules and mirrors the Give Vassal Directive interaction exactly. It only ever does what you could do by hand.
[*][b]Load this mod after A Game of Thrones[/b] (below it in your playset), so an exempt vassal's directive icon still shows dimmed. That icon is drawn by a text function A Game of Thrones also defines, so whichever mod loads last wins. Loaded before AGOT, everything else still works; only the dimming is lost.
[*][b]DLC[/b]: none required. The Free City directives come from Roads to Power, which A Game of Thrones uses for its administrative realms; without it the panel quietly hides them.
[*][b]Achievements still work.[/b] Since 1.9, mods do not disable them.
[*][b]Safe to add to an existing save[/b], and safe to remove from one.
[*][b]Multiplayer[/b]: every player gets their own rules, on their own character.
[*][b]The AI is untouched.[/b] Your rules are yours; AI rulers manage their own vassals as always.
[*][b]Every official language[/b] is supported. The English is written by hand; the rest are machine-translated, so please forgive any stiffness, and corrections are welcome.
[/list]

[b]Warning:[/b] while automation is on, the rules can overwrite a directive you set by hand on a vassal the mod is not already managing. But change a directive the automation itself gave a vassal, and it exempts them for you, so your choice stands. To protect a vassal from the start, exempt them yourself.

I am one person with a real job, so please be patient if a patch breaks something. Bug reports are welcome. An [i]error.log[/i] excerpt and what you expected goes a long way. Contributions are very welcome too, including fixes to the machine-made translations.

[b]Requires:[/b] Crusader Kings III 1.19.x and the A Game of Thrones mod. No DLC is needed.
